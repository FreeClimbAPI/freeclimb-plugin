# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is business-minded FreeClimb stakeholders evaluating how AI agents can make FreeClimb easier to adopt, demo, and build with. They are not expected to understand MCP, PerCL, or CLI internals before seeing the value.

## Product Purpose

This presentation demonstrates that FreeClimb's flexibility becomes an advantage when AI agents have the right product knowledge, tools, and guardrails. Success means the audience understands the business value: a shorter path from a customer idea to a working communications workflow.

## Brand Personality

Confident, practical, and agent-ready. The tone should feel like a clear business demo from a technical company: energetic enough for a marketing surface, grounded enough to be credible.

## Anti-references

Do not make the deck feel like an internal dashboard, generic SaaS pitch, terminal-native hacker aesthetic, or dense architecture review. Avoid repeated generic cards, tiny uppercase labels on every section as the main visual grammar, vague AI hype, and diagrams that require technical explanation before the value is clear.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with business value before implementation details.
2. Show FreeClimb as a communications layer AI agents can understand and operate.
3. Make the plugin feel like the packaging for FreeClimb expertise, not just another tool.
4. Use bold brand energy without losing credibility.
5. Keep every slide easy to explain in one sentence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use high-contrast text, readable type sizes, keyboard-operable navigation, and reduced-motion-safe transitions. Avoid relying on color alone to communicate structure or status.
